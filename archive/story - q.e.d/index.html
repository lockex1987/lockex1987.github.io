38 Empty Dream
17		 17 - During the Edo period (1600's to 1850's), certain people across Japan dabbled in the early development of differential calculus as an intellectual pastime, called "wasan" (Japanese algebra). However, rather than finding practical real-world applications for it, wasan masters would paint their problems and solutions on votive boards ("sangaku") to be donated to shinto shrines. This chapter discusses wasan, sangaku and the potential independent discovery of the math concept of "i".
